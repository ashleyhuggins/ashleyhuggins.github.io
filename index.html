Game Summary

The Florida Gators took on the Georgia Bulldogs on the afternoon of October 27th. Set to beautiful weather in Jacksonville, this bitter rivalry was nothing short of what was expected. Behind a stout defense that gave Georgia headaches all afternoon, the Gators did enough on offense to hold the Bulldogs off. After taking an early 3-0 lead in the first quarter, Georgia looked like they might have been putting the Gators on upset alert. However, Florida was quick to respond with a touchdown of their own. The teams headed into halftine with a score of 13-10, still a close matchup.

The Bulldogs received the second half kickoff and were immediately met with a stout defensive effort. Freshman quarterback Jacob Eason was finding it hard to throw with the Gators defensive front swarming in. The running game also had their struggles with renowned running back Nick Chubb being held to a measly 20 yards. With Florida's defense holding the Dawgs, their offense was able to score two second half touchdowns that put the game out of reach.

With this win, Florida remained at the top of the SEC East, and Georgia was left with the 4th loss of their last 5 games. Next week the Bulldogs head to Kentucky to try and right the ship. Florida pays Arkansas a visit in a non-divsion matchup to keep a firm grip on the top spot of the East.
